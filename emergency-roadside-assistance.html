FAQ

My car just broke down, what should I do?

First off, do not panic and stay calm, car breakdowns are very common.

If your car breaks down, follow these steps:

  1. Stay Safe: If possible, move your car to the side of the road or a safe location away from traffic. Turn on your hazard lights to alert other drivers.
  2. Assess the Situation: Try to identify the problem with your car. If you 're unsure or suspect a major issue, it 's best not to attempt repairs yourself.
  3. Call for Roadside Assistance: Contact your roadside assistance provider immediately. They will dispatch help to your location and assist with jump-starting your car, providing fuel, changing a flat tire, or arranging for towing if necessary.
  4. Communicate Clearly: When calling for assistance, provide your exact location, the nature of the problem, and any other relevant details. This will help the service provider send the appropriate help.
  5. Wait for Help: Stay patient and wait for the roadside assistance team to arrive. They will work to get your car running again or tow it to a nearby repair facility if needed.

Remember, roadside assistance is there to help you in times of car trouble, so consider purchasing a reliable provider 's roadside assistance coverage plan .

Why wouldn 't my car start?

There are several reasons why your car may not start. Some common causes include:

  1. Dead Battery: The most common reason for a car not starting is a dead or weak battery. This can happen due to leaving lights or electronics on for an extended period, an aging battery (over a year old), or a faulty charging system.
  2. Ignition Issues: Problems with the ignition switch, ignition coil, or spark plugs can prevent the engine from starting.
  3. Fuel System Problems: Issues with the fuel pump, fuel filter, or fuel injectors can result in a lack of fuel delivery to the engine, causing starting problems.
  4. Starter Motor Failure: A faulty starter motor may prevent the engine from cranking.
  5. Electrical Problems: Electrical issues, such as a blown fuse or malfunctioning electrical components, can disrupt the starting process.
  6. Mechanical Failure: Internal engine problems or mechanical issues can lead to starting difficulties.
  7. Transmission in "Park ": If your car has an automatic transmission, make sure it is in the "Park "or "Neutral "position before attempting to start the engine.

If your car doesn 't start, it 's best to contact a professional mechanic or your roadside assistance provider to diagnose and resolve the issue. They can perform necessary repairs or tow your car to a repair shop if needed.

Avoid attempting complex repairs on your own, as it may worsen the problem or void your vehicle 's warranty.

What should I do if my car breaks down in a remote or rural area?

If your car breaks down in a remote or rural area, prioritise your safety and follow these steps: 

  1. Pull your vehicle over to the side of the road if possible and turn on hazard lights.
  2. Stay inside your vehicle, especially if you 're in an unfamiliar or isolated area.
  3. Call for fast-response roadside assistance. If you 're unable to make a call, raise your vehicle 's hood or tie a bright cloth to the antenna to signal for help.
  4. If someone stops to offer assistance, remain inside your vehicle and ask them to call for help on your behalf.
  5. Avoid accepting rides from strangers and wait for professional assistance to arrive.

Call a roadside assistance service provider to come to fix your vehicle or tow it to safety. Fast-response roadside assistance is advisable here, especially those that can arrive at your location within minutes.

I locked myself out of my car... help!

If you 've accidentally locked yourself out of your car, don 't panic. Follow these steps to get back in:

  1. Check for Spare Key: First, see if you have a spare key with you or if someone you trust can bring it to you.
  2. Call Roadside Assistance: Call for roadside assistance, they can send a locksmith to unlock your car.
  3. Use a Clothes Hanger or Shoelace (Caution!): In some cases, you may be able to unlock the car yourself using a hanger or shoelace. However, this method can damage your car and is not recommended unless you have experience with this technique.
  4. Contact a Locksmith: If you don 't have roadside assistance or cannot unlock the car yourself, call a reputable locksmith. Some are trained to unlock vehicles without causing damage.
  5. Stay Safe: While waiting for help, stay in a safe location, especially if you 're stranded in an unfamiliar area or during inclement weather.

It 's important to avoid using any sharp objects or tools that could damage your car 's windows or locks.

Calling for professional help from a locksmith or roadside assistance provider is the safest and most effective way to regain access to your vehicle.

Should I change the flat tyre myself?

Changing a flat tyre can be done if you feel confident and have the necessary tools and knowledge. Here are some considerations:

Pros of Changing Your Own Flat Tyre:

  1. Cost Savings: Changing the tyre yourself can save you money on service fees.
  2. Convenience: If you 're skilled and prepared, it can be quicker than waiting for roadside assistance.
  3. Empowerment: Knowing how to change a tyre is a valuable skill that can make you more self-reliant on the road. And you 'll have an exciting story to tell too!
Cons of Changing Your Own Flat Tyre:
  1. Safety Risk: Changing a tyre on the side of the road can be hazardous, especially in traffic.
  2. Lack of Experience: If you 're not familiar with the process, you could damage the car or not secure the tyre properly.
  3. Physical Effort: Changing a tyre requires physical strength and may not be feasible for everyone.
  4. Weather Conditions: Singapore 's hot and wet weather can make the process more challenging and uncomfortable.

Ultimately, the decision to change your own flat tyre depends on your comfort level, experience, and the safety of the situation.

If you 're uncertain or feel unsafe, it 's best to call for roadside assistance . Professional technicians are equipped to handle tyre changes safely and efficiently, ensuring your safety and getting you back on the road quickly.

Does Roadside Assistance includes repairs?

Roadside assistance typically includes minor repairs that can be done on the spot to get your vehicle moving again. Here are some common types of repairs that roadside assistance may cover:

  1. Battery Jump-Start: If your car 's battery is dead, roadside assistance can provide a jump-start to get it running.
  2. Flat Tyre Assistance: Roadside assistance can help with changing a flat tyre and installing your spare tyre.
  3. Fuel Delivery: If you run out of fuel, roadside assistance can provide enough petrol or diesel to get you to the nearest petrol station.
  4. Lockout Service: If you lock yourself out of your car, roadside assistance can help unlock the doors.
  5. Minor Mechanical Repairs: Some plans may include minor repairs like fixing a loose battery cable or a broken belt.

Major repairs that require extensive work or replacement of parts may not be possible, and in such cases, the provider may arrange for towing to a nearby repair facility.
